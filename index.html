GST Related Questions

Q: What is GST?
A: GST stands for Goods and Services Tax, a comprehensive tax levied on the supply of goods and services in India.
Q: How do I register for GST?
A: We can assist you with the GST registration process. Please contact us for detailed guidance and support.
Q: What documents are required for GST registration?
A: Common documents required include PAN card, Aadhaar card, proof of business registration, and bank account details.
Q: How long does it take to get GST registration?
A: It typically takes about 7-10 working days if all documents are in order.
Q: What are the benefits of GST registration?
A: Benefits include legal recognition, input tax credit, ease of interstate business, and improved tax compliance.

ITR Filing Questions

Q: What is ITR?
A: ITR stands for Income Tax Return, a form used to declare your income and tax details to the Income Tax Department.
Q: Who needs to file an ITR?
A: Individuals, HUFs, companies, and firms with taxable income or financial transactions need to file ITR.
Q: When is the last date to file ITR?
A: The due date for filing ITR is usually July 31st for individuals and September 30th for companies.
Q: What documents are needed for ITR filing?
A: Documents required include Form 16, TDS certificates, bank statements, investment proofs, and other income-related documents.
Q: Can I file my ITR myself?
A: Yes, but professional help ensures accuracy and maximizes your tax benefits. We offer expert ITR filing services.

TDS Management Questions

Q: What is TDS?
A: TDS stands for Tax Deducted at Source, a means of collecting income tax in India by deducting it from the source of income.
Q: How is TDS deducted?
A: TDS is deducted by the payer while making specified payments such as salary, commission, rent, etc.
Q: What is the TDS rate?
A: TDS rates vary based on the type of payment and the recipient’s status. Please contact us for specific rates.
Q: How do I file a TDS return?
A: We can assist with the preparation and filing of TDS returns. Please get in touch for detailed assistance.
Q: What happens if TDS is not deducted?
A: Non-deduction of TDS can lead to penalties and interest. It’s crucial to comply with TDS regulations.
